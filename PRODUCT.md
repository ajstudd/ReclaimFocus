# Product

## Register

product

## Users

ReclaimFocus is for students, remote workers, professionals, and anyone trying to reduce digital distraction while staying in control of their own browsing environment. They use the extension in short, high-intent moments: blocking a site, setting a timer, scheduling a reminder, checking activity, or opening Zen Mode before returning to work.

## Product Purpose

ReclaimFocus helps people reclaim attention by blocking distracting websites, filtering search keywords, limiting time on selected domains, scheduling reminders, and offering a calm Zen Mode reset. Success means the extension feels local-first, trustworthy, quick to configure, and clear enough that discipline feels like a designed environment rather than punishment.

## Brand Personality

Premium, calm, disciplined. The voice should be direct and encouraging without shame. The interface should feel like a refined productivity instrument: confident, private, focused, and quietly motivating.

## Anti-references

Do not make the product feel like a generic SaaS dashboard, a gamified habit tracker, a loud dopamine-blocking app, or a decorative wellness page. Avoid overbearing purple gradients, noisy motivational copy, vague empty states, and schedule controls that expose implementation details before the user has chosen what kind of reminder they want.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with intent: the user should choose what they are trying to do before seeing detailed controls.
2. Make discipline feel humane: copy and states should guide without scolding.
3. Preserve local trust: privacy, control, and reversibility should be visible through calm UI affordances.
4. Prefer clock-app familiarity: scheduling should behave like alarms and reminders people already understand.
5. Stay dense but legible: the popup is small, so hierarchy and spacing must help users act fast.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Support keyboard focus states, clear labels, reduced-motion preferences, color-independent status cues, and readable compact text in light and dark modes.
